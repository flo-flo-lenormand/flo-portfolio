# About Page - Final Draft

I'm Flo. I've been designing message bubbles for a while now.

It started with safety. My job was to make sure conversations stay safe. I designed systems that stop the worst from happening before someone has to live through it. That team at Meta taught me that the most important design work is the kind nobody ever sees.

Then I moved to media, crafting how people's photos and videos show up in conversations. Their content deserves to look better in a chat than in their camera roll. We once spent weeks building a physics engine just for an emoji reaction animation. It was a small thing. But that animation is what made leadership care about the entire project. Craft isn't decoration. It's how you get people to pay attention.

Now I'm at MSL. Robots are entering the chat, and I'm still figuring out what that should feel like.

I got into design the unusual way. French literature at the Sorbonne, then product design at EnsAD in Paris. My first job in San Francisco was at SpotAngels, a tiny parking app. I came back as their only designer and we got featured by Apple three times in one year. My parents were very proud, which is the only metric that matters.

I build to think. I prototype with SwiftUI, Next.js, and Claude Code because the best way to explain an idea is to let someone touch it. I also run "Designers Ship Diffs" at Meta, where I teach designers to write and ship production code.

Outside of work I live in France with my partner Emma (also a designer, which means we argue about spacing at dinner) and our daughter Michelle. I surf when there are waves, hike when there aren't, and occasionally build AI agents that sell my family's old clothes online.

If any of this resonates, I'm @flo_lenormand on Threads or you can email me.
