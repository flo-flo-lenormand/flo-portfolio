# Frame: Problem Definition

## The Ask
Rewrite the portfolio site content so it sounds more like Flo and communicates the seniority and thoughtfulness of his design work.

## The Real Problem
The current content is good but it undersells Flo. It reads like a competent designer who happens to work at Meta - not like a design leader who's shaped how billions of people interact with messaging, safety, and AI agents. The descriptions are modest to the point of being flat. The article summaries explain *what* each piece is about, but not *why it matters* or what thinking sits behind it. There's no connective thread - no sense of what Flo actually cares about as a designer or where his work is going.

Specifically:

1. **The homepage intro is generic.** "Product Designer at Meta, designing AI agents. I write about design, AI, and the things I get wrong." This could be anyone at Meta. It doesn't convey a point of view, a design philosophy, or the depth of experience behind it. "The things I get wrong" is self-deprecating in a way that undercuts authority.

2. **Article descriptions are surface-level.** They describe the topic but not the insight. "Being able to generate something is not the same as understanding if it's good" - this is fine, but it's the kind of thing anyone could say. The descriptions don't make a reader think "this person sees things I don't."

3. **The About page is chronological, not thematic.** It's a resume in paragraph form: I worked here, then here, then here. It doesn't communicate *how* Flo thinks about design or what drives him. The humor is nice (the parents line, the spacing argument) but it carries the emotional weight alone - the professional substance underneath is thin.

4. **The meta description / SEO copy repeats the homepage problem.** Same generic line.

5. **The tone is already close to Flo's voice** - conversational, human, no corporate jargon. That part is working. What's missing is *weight*. The voice is right but the content doesn't carry enough substance to signal seniority.

## Why It Matters
A portfolio is often the first impression for potential collaborators, recruiters, speaking invitations, and cross-org partners. Right now this reads as "nice designer who writes." It should read as "design leader with a specific worldview who builds things and thinks about them rigorously." The gap between those two impressions is the difference between someone bookmarking the site and someone closing the tab.

## Success Criteria
- Every piece of copy communicates a point of view, not just a fact
- A reader should finish the homepage thinking "this person thinks differently about design"
- The About page should feel like meeting Flo, not reading his resume
- Article descriptions should create curiosity through insight, not topic summary
- Tone stays human and conversational - never corporate, never self-important
- Passes the voice spec: no triple-adjective lists, no em dashes, no selling, no AI tells
- Seniority comes through in the *thinking*, not in title-dropping or credential-listing

## Quality Bar for Evaluate
1. **Voice check:** Could Flo send this without edits? Does it pass the anti-AI patterns test?
2. **Authority check:** Does it communicate staff-level thinking without being self-congratulatory?
3. **Curiosity check:** Would a design leader at another company want to keep reading?
4. **Coherence check:** Is there a through-line across all copy - homepage, about, descriptions?
5. **Humility check:** Does it stay grounded? Flo's humor and self-awareness should survive the upgrade.

## Scope
**In scope:** Homepage intro, meta description, About page copy, all 7 article descriptions, contact line.
**Out of scope:** Article body content (the actual essays), site structure/design, nav labels.
