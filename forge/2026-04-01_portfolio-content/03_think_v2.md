## The real through-line: Designing Conversations

This is the brand.

Not "messaging designer" (too narrow, sounds like a niche). Not "communication expert" (too corporate). But someone who has spent their career designing how people talk to each other - and now, how people talk to machines.

**Why this works:**
- It's factually true and defensible (6 years Messenger, now MSL agents)
- It's forward-looking (conversation design with AI is the frontier)
- It's differentiated (very few designers have this specific depth in conversation UX)
- It connects the old work to the new work seamlessly
- It makes the craft obsession concrete (craft in conversation = the micro-interactions, the timing, the physics of a reaction animation, the feel of typing indicators)
- It makes the AI experimentation relevant (he's not just playing with AI, he's designing how humans and AI will talk to each other)

**The bigger story:** Flo has been designing conversations for years. First between people. Now between people and AI. The thing he cares about hasn't changed - how do you make a conversation feel right? The medium changed (text -> media -> agents) but the core question is the same.

## Brand Architecture

### Core positioning
**"I design conversations."**

Simple. Covers the full career. Opens up naturally to messaging, AI agents, and the future.

### Supporting pillars

1. **Craft in the details** - The physics animation that made people care about reactions. The prototype that changed how leadership saw video controls. Not claiming mastery, but showing the obsession. "I think the best work happens in the details people skip."

2. **Thinking before building** - Every article is about this tension. Built 18 features then realized the problem didn't exist. Built a dashboard then deleted it. Used AI for a sprint and it worked but felt wrong. The brand says: I build fast AND I know when to stop.

3. **AI as a thinking partner, not a replacement** - He uses AI constantly but also builds tools to make AI disagree with him (/roast). He writes critically about AI's limits. This is a nuanced position that's rare and valuable.

### The domain question

If the brand is "designing conversations," the domain should reinforce that. Options to explore:
- Something with "conversation" or "talk" or "dialogue" in it
- Or lean into the personal name with authority (flolenormand.com is clean and professional)
- Or something unexpected that signals the POV (harder to execute, higher risk)

This is worth discussing with Flo - the domain is a branding decision.

## Approaches to the "Designing Conversations" brand

### Approach A: Direct statement
"I've spent the last ten years designing how people talk to each other. First on Messenger. Now with AI."

Pros: Clear, confident, immediately differentiating.
Cons: Could feel like a tagline. Needs substance around it.

### Approach B: Question-led
"What makes a conversation feel right? I've been working on that question for ten years."

Pros: Invites curiosity. Positions Flo as a thinker.
Cons: Could feel vague without the supporting content.

### Approach C: Craft-forward
"I design the moments between messages - the typing indicator, the reaction animation, the pause before an AI responds."

Pros: Immediately shows craft obsession. Concrete and sensory.
Cons: Might be too narrow. Focuses on micro-interactions at the expense of strategic thinking.

### Approach D: Narrative arc
"I started designing messaging between people. Now I'm designing messaging between people and AI. The question is the same: how do you make it feel human?"

Pros: Shows the career arc, the transition, and the core belief in one breath.
Cons: Longer. Might be too much for a homepage intro.

## Recommendation

**Approach D for the About page, Approach A for the homepage intro.**

The homepage should be sharp and fast. The About page has room for the full narrative. The article descriptions bridge the two by showing the thinking in action.

The domain name and meta description should reinforce "designing conversations" without being literal about it. More on that in the Craft stage.
