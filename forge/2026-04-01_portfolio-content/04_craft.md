## 3. About Page

**Current:** Four paragraphs, chronological. Resume in prose form.

**New:**

---

I've been designing the same thing for ten years: what happens inside a message bubble.

It started with safety. I spent three years at Meta designing systems that protect people from scams, harassment, and unwanted contact on Messenger and Instagram. The work was invisible when it worked, which is exactly how it should be. That team taught me that the best design often means someone never sees a problem at all.

Then I moved to media. I redesigned how photos and videos live inside conversations - the way you react to an image, the way a video feels when you open it. We once spent weeks building a physics engine for an emoji reaction animation. It was a small thing. But that animation is what made leadership care about the entire project. I learned that craft isn't decoration. It's how you get people to pay attention.

Now I'm at Meta Superintelligence Labs, designing AI agent experiences. The message bubble is the same. The thing inside it changed. Instead of a photo or a text from a friend, it might be a response from an agent that just booked your flight. The question I keep asking is the same one I've always asked: how do you make this feel right?

I got into design the unusual way. French literature at the Sorbonne, then product design at EnsAD in Paris. My first job in San Francisco was at SpotAngels, a tiny parking app. I came back as their only designer and we got featured by Apple three times in one year. My parents were very proud, which is the only metric that matters.

I build to think. I prototype with SwiftUI, Next.js, and Claude Code because the best way to explain an idea is to let someone touch it. I also run "Designers Ship Diffs" at Meta, where I teach designers to write and ship production code. And I write about the tension between building fast and thinking clearly - mostly because I keep getting it wrong and learning something each time.

Outside of work I live in France with my partner Emma (also a designer, which means we argue about spacing at dinner) and our daughter Michelle. I surf when there are waves, hike when there aren't, and occasionally build AI agents that sell my family's old clothes online.

---

**What changed:**
- Organized by the three brand chapters (safe, expressive, smart) instead of chronology
- Each chapter has a specific lesson learned, not just a job description
- The craft story (physics engine for emoji) is front and center as proof
- "Build to think" replaces a list of tools with a philosophy
- The personal section is unchanged - it already works
- "I keep getting it wrong and learning something each time" replaces "the things I get wrong" - same humility, more active, more senior

## 6. Domain Name Options

The current domain situation isn't visible in the code, but here are options that reinforce the brand:

**Name-based (safest, most professional):**
- flolenormand.com - clean, searchable, works forever
- flo.design - short, memorable, signals the profession

**Brand-reinforcing:**
- messagebubbles.design - bold, memorable, directly tied to the brand line
- bubbles.fyi - playful, short, the .fyi TLD matches the writing-first approach (like laurent.fyi)

**Conceptual:**
- feltright.design - from the core question "how do you make this feel right?"

**My recommendation:** Either `flo.design` (if available - short, professional, ages well) or `bubbles.fyi` (if you want to commit to the brand line and match Laurent's .fyi pattern). The brand is strong enough that a playful domain reinforces it rather than undermining it.
